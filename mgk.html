Section 1
Medicare 101: The Four Parts

Medicare is the federal health insurance program for Americans 65+ and certain people under 65 with qualifying disabilities.

PartCovers2026 Cost
A — HospitalInpatient, SNF, hospice$0/mo. Ded: $1,736
B — MedicalDoctors, outpatient, DME$202.90/mo. $283 ded.
C — AdvantageBundles A+B+D privatelyOften $0. MOOP $9,350
D — DrugsPrescription coverageAvg $38.99/mo. Cap $2K
⚠️ The 20% Problem: Part B pays 80% — you pay 20% with NO annual cap. A $500K cancer treatment = $100K personal liability.
Section 2
Enrollment Windows & Timelines

Strict windows with permanent consequences for missing them. Penalties are added every month for the rest of your Medicare life.

IEP (7-month): 3 months before, birthday month, 3 months after 65. Miss it = 10% Part B penalty per year late — forever.
Medigap OE (One-Time Only): 6 months after Part B. Guaranteed issue. Irreplaceable.
SEP: 8 months after active employer coverage ends. 63 days for Part D.
AEP: Oct 15–Dec 7. Switch Advantage or Part D plans.
🚨 COBRA Trap: NOT creditable for Part D. Penalty clock starts from active employer coverage end — not COBRA end.
Section 3
Medicare Supplement (Medigap)

Supplement plans fill Medicare's gaps. Benefits are standardized by federal law — every carrier offers identical coverage for each plan letter. Only the price differs.

Plan GPlan N
100% Part B coinsurance (after $283 ded.)100% coinsurance (small copays)
Part A deductible ($1,736) coveredPart A deductible covered
Does NOT cover Part B deductibleDoes NOT cover excess charges
💡 Same Plan G can vary 20–50% in premium between carriers in the same ZIP. An independent agent compares every carrier.
Section 4
Medicare Advantage (Part C)

Bundles A, B, and usually D through private insurers. Often $0 premium with dental, vision, and hearing benefits. Trade-off: networks and prior authorizations.

⚠️ Plans can change benefits, networks, and formularies every October 1. Annual reviews with a licensed agent are essential.
Section 5
Part D: Prescription Drug Coverage

Part D covers drugs through tiered formularies. Late enrollment penalty: 1% × $38.99 × months uncovered, rounded to nearest $0.10 — permanent.

TierDrug TypeCost
1 — Pref. GenericCommon generics$0–$5
2 — GenericStandard generics$5–$20
3 — Pref. BrandCommon brand-name$30–$60
5 — SpecialtyBiologics, oncology25–33%
24 months = $9.40/mo forever. 60 months = $23.40/mo. Enroll even with no prescriptions.
2025+: $2,000 annual OOP cap — catastrophic gap eliminated.
Section 6
IRMAA: Income Surcharges

Extra charge on Part B and Part D for higher-income beneficiaries. Based on income 2 years prior — 2026 surcharge = 2024 MAGI.

2024 Single MAGIPart B +Annual +
≤ $109,000$0$0
$109K–$137K$81.20$1,149
$137K–$171K$202.90$2,886
$171K–$205K$324.60$4,620
> $500,000$487.00$6,936
SSA-44 Appeal: Retirement, reduced hours, or death of spouse = appeal using more recent, lower income — often effective immediately.
Section 7
Medicare & Working Americans

Employer size is the single most critical Medicare question for active workers. Getting it wrong = permanent penalties.

Employer SizeMedicareAction
Under 20 employeesPRIMARYEnroll at 65 regardless
20+ employeesSECONDARYMay delay penalty-free
HSA Conflict: Stop contributions 6 months before Medicare enrollment.

Day employer coverage ends: SEP (8 months Part B) + 63-day Part D clock — both start simultaneously.

Medicare handles healthcare costs. Annuities solve the income problem — guaranteed income that cannot run out no matter how long you live.

Section 9
Long-Term Care: The Gap Medicare Won't Cover

Medicare does NOT cover custodial care. 70% of Americans will need it. Medicare covers max 100 SNF days after a qualifying hospital stay.

Care TypeAnnual Cost
Private nursing home$115,000+/yr
Assisted living$68,000/yr
In-home aide (44 hrs/wk)$75,000/yr
Hybrid LTC/Life (most popular): Single premium, 2–4× LTC benefit, death benefit if unused.
Deposit $75K–$150K, get 2–4× in LTC benefits. Unused = death benefit to heirs. Solves use-it-or-lose-it.

Section 11
Hospital Indemnity & Critical Illness

Cash-pay products that pay directly to you — not a provider. No network restrictions.

Hospital Indemnity: $250–$400/day per hospital day. Offsets MA copays. $40–$100/mo.
Critical Illness: $10K–$50K lump sum on diagnosis of heart attack, stroke, cancer, kidney failure, or organ transplant. $30–$65/mo.
Accident: ER visits, fractures, ambulance. $15–$35/mo.
MA + Hospital Indemnity bundle: $0 MA premium + $50–$80/mo indemnity = near-zero hospital exposure.
